2026
====

**2 July — New article on vaccine hesitancy.** My article, “Conspiracy Theories Are Central to the Complex System of Predictors of Italian Vaccine Hesitancy,” was published in *Italian Political Science*. [Read the article](https://italianpoliticalscience.com/index.php/ips/article/view/295).

**February — New LONELY-EU research resource.** With Katarzyna Growiec, Maike Luhmann, Yiming Tang, and Cristiano Vezzoni, I released the *LONELY-EU WP3_1A: Database of Loneliness Datasets in Europe*. [View the report and database](https://doi.org/10.17605/OSF.IO/3T9KG).

**6 February — SUSPECTS project article published.** Our overview of the SUSPECTS comparative research programme appeared in *Italian Political Science*. [Read the article](https://doi.org/10.69101/IPS.2025.20.1.4).

**New work on political participation.** I presented “Disconnected Citizens: Social Isolation, Loneliness, and Voter Turnout in Europe” at a LONELY-EU ambassador meeting and at the project’s internal seminar.

**Teaching.** I taught the Applied Statistics Lab for MA students at Collegio Carlo Alberto, using R.

2025
====

**4 December — Beyond constraint.** “Beyond Constraint: Tightness and Consensus of Political Belief Systems” was published online in *Italian Political Science Review*. [Read the open-access article](https://doi.org/10.1017/ipo.2025.10080).

**1 August — Anger and inequality belief systems.** “Consolidation and Change” was published online in *Network Science*. [Read the open-access article](https://doi.org/10.1017/nws.2025.10004).

**July — Comparative conspiracy-theory measurement.** Co-authored work on cross-national measurement was presented at the International Society of Political Psychology Annual Meeting in Prague and at the AssoComPol conference in Milan.

**Invited presentation in Salzburg.** I presented comparative research on conspiracy beliefs, conspiracist mentality, and political ideology at the University of Salzburg’s Department of Political Science.

**New position.** After completing my PhD in Political Science, I joined the University of Milan as a postdoctoral researcher on the Horizon Europe LONELY-EU project.

2024
====

**Inequality belief systems.** “Inequality Belief Systems: What They Look Like, How to Study Them, and Why They Matter” was published in *Social Indicators Research*. [Read the open-access article](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11205-024-03352-5).

**Belief systems as networks.** I presented my research at the INSNA Sunbelt Conference in Edinburgh, at the University of Milan-Bicocca, and at Behave Rendez-vous in Milan. I also delivered an invited MA lecture at the University of Milan.
