We treat beliefs about inequality as a structured system rather than a bundle of separate opinions, and show how to recover that structure from survey data by combining Correlational Class Analysis with Exploratory Graph Analysis. Tested on original US and Dutch data, the framework reveals cohesive, country-specific belief networks centred on perceptions of inequality and support for redistribution.
