Vaccine hesitancy is usually explained through long inventories of separate correlates. Modelling it instead as a network of interdependent determinants on Italian data, conspiracy endorsement emerges as a central structuring element linked to most other variables, while only a limited set of factors remain directly tied to hesitancy net of the rest, pointing to sharper intervention targets.
