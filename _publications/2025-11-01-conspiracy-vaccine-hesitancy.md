Rather than treating the predictors of vaccine hesitancy as a list of independent correlates, the article reconstructs them as an interconnected system. Beliefs about the origin of COVID-19 that were considered conspiratorial when the survey was fielded emerge as a central node associated with many other predictors, while a narrower set of variables remains directly related to hesitancy after accounting for the rest of the network.

In this paper, **conspiracy beliefs** refers specifically to respondents' beliefs about the origin of COVID-19. In the context of data collection, between March and June 2021, the relevant response options expressed rejection of the dominant public-health account and greater openness to alternative interpretations that were then commonly classified as conspiratorial. The article does not evaluate whether those claims were ultimately true or false.
