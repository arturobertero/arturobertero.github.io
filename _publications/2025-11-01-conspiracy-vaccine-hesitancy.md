Rather than treating the predictors of vaccine hesitancy as a list of independent correlates, the article reconstructs them as an interconnected system. Conspiracy endorsement emerges as a central node associated with many other predictors, while a narrower set of variables remains directly related to hesitancy after accounting for the rest of the network.
